Not a chatbot

An integrated study partner inside the learning journey

TenTen lives where learning actually breaks — general chat, recorded lessons, exam review, and live class. It knows each student's class, grade, and curriculum context so answers stay personalized, not generic.

The problem

When learning paused, students had nowhere to turn

Stuck mid-lesson on a recorded class. Confused after an exam explanation. Half-formed Bangla questions about homework or platform basics. These everyday moments needed a study partner — not another static FAQ page.

From pilot to product

What I built

R&D

Early prototypes in Colab

Started retrieval and prompt experiments ~10 months before launch — testing Bangla Q&A, chunking strategies, and answer quality before production wiring.

RAG

Curriculum-grounded retrieval

Built pipelines to pull evidence from textbooks and trusted 10MS content when students ask academic doubts — with evaluation loops to track retrieval quality.

Routing

Mixed-intent handling

Not every question maps to a book chunk. Routed operational queries — class schedules, 10MS FAQs, platform help — separately from subject-matter RAG.

Analytics

Post-launch measurement

Product analytics on usage, retention, and answer helpfulness so improvements stay data-led after limited release in Nov '25.

Engineering challenges

Bangla understanding

Base models often misread Bangla phrasing, code-mixing, and informal student language — requiring iterative prompt and eval work, not one-shot prompts.

Ambiguous retrieval

Student questions are vague; textbook chunks are dense. Matching the right passage from curriculum content was one of the hardest RAG problems we faced.

Beyond textbook intent

“When is my next class?” and general 10MS questions need different tooling than “explain this physics step” — intent routing was essential.
